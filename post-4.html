CheshireMitty· 14d ago

I have been thinking about this a lot and I think there is a really strong argument that either we are living in a simulation or there are no truly superintelligent beings in the universe. Hear me out.

Imagine there is a civilization somewhere that is insanely advanced, like post-biological, fully digital, capable of running simulations inside simulations, basically god level smart. If they existed, they would be able to see the consequences of every human decision, past and future. They would understand which suffering is part of learning, like falling off a bike or getting blisters from running too fast, versus suffering that is totally unnecessary, preventable, and catastrophic, like genocide, starvation, and systemic oppression.

Here is the thing. Humans have had thousands of years of trial and error and we still keep messing up in big ways. Slavery was abolished, but economic systems today keep people trapped in debt, minimum wage jobs they cannot survive on, and forced to rely on loans and credit cards just to live. Racism is not gone; in some ways it is more overt now than ever. Politically, we even elect people who actively hurt others while claiming it is law and order. If a superintelligent being were watching us, they would see all this and understand that letting us figure it out ourselves is more damaging than helpful.

Think of it like zookeepers. We do not let dominant animals just kill each other when it is unnecessary. We intervene to keep the system stable. Humanity is one species on one planet. A truly advanced intelligence could see the long term effects of our conflicts and failures the same way we see a lion fight going wrong. They would not let it run its course unless there was some bigger purpose because the cost is too high.

Since we do not see any intervention, there are really only two options.

1. There is no superintelligent being capable of intervening. If one existed, they would absolutely have to step in when humans hit moral thresholds we clearly fail at.

2. We are in a simulation. The suffering and lack of intervention are allowed deliberately, maybe to observe outcomes, and that explains why nothing stops catastrophic events.

It is mind blowing when you think about it. Either we are alone in the universe in terms of moral superintelligence or everything we see is part of a controlled experiment. Either way, the fact that slavery, systemic oppression, and preventable suffering still exist on this planet is evidence that humans cannot be trusted to learn on their own, not even after thousands of years of progress.

I would love to hear what others think. Is this a compelling way to frame the Fermi paradox in moral terms or am I missing something?
