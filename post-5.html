Why does Psytrance, unlike other EDM genres, sound so much better at festivals and clubs than at home?

I noticed that Psytrance played at festivals and clubs sounds so much better than Psytrance played at home. The same thing isn't true for other EDM genres, like Techno and Free Tekno for example, there is still a huge difference but not so massive.

I will explain better what I mean, when I listen to Psytrance played at festivals and clubs I can hear the melodies, the chords, the basslines and the beats. When I play Psytrance at home it seems like the speakers, the earbuds, the headphones are too cheap to reproduce all these layers (melodies, chords, basslines, beats) at the same time and they blend and merge all of them together penalizing basslines and beats, especially kicks, kicks become barely audible. The sound structure of the track seems like it is always in breakdown (it seems like there are just melodies and chords without basslines and beats), except during the intro and outro, where there are less layers and basslines and kicks are less penalized by the cheap speakers, earbuds and headphone. Due to this it's not enjoyable at all listening to Psytrance at home. Listening to Psytrance at festivals and clubs instead is VERY enjoyable, it is like a life changing experience.

My theory is that other EDM genres, like Techno and Free Tekno mentioned before, are more raw than Psytrance, they are simpler, they have less layers and for that reason listening to them at home through cheap speakers, earbuds and headphones produces an experience that is less distant than festivals and clubs. There is still a huge difference, I don't want to say at all that listening to them at home is similar to listening to them at festivals and clubs, just that the distance is not so far as with Psytrance.

Am I correct in my theory? Is Psytrance more penalized than other EDM genres if listened to it through cheap speakers, earbuds and headphone? Do you too experience this downgrade with Psytrance if you listen to it through cheap speakers, earbuds and headphones where all the layers blend and merge and kicks become barely audible?

pieter3d· 3y ago

Good headphones/speakers certainly help. The cheapest and most reliable option is studio headphones. Beyerdynamic DT770 pro's are a very good option. You won't find anything better unless you're willing to spent several times as much. Don't bother with headphones marketed towards general consumers if you're mainly after sound quality.

A nice stereo setup is great too, but it's not going to sound as good unless you also treat your room and turn up the volume to the point where it might annoy the neighbors.

Another thing is simply volume and dynamic range. Loud music sounds better and loud/deep bass makes you want to dance. You're not going to get that at home, unless you live in a very remote area and have lots of disposable income. Furthermore, you need good acoustics and a room with the right dimensions if you want a clean mix with deep bass. At good clubs they spent a lot of time to tune the sound specifically for that room. Then there's the thing that the music is being mixed live specifically for that situation.

At home you generally only get a mix that's made so that it sounds acceptable on shitty speakers/earbuds/car stereos, because that's what most people use. That's often not the best mix for good headphones/speakers. The more mainstream music suffers the most from this in my experience. That said, the psytrance I like sounds pretty amazing on my good stereo and headphones
